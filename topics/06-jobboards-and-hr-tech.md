# Jobboards, HR Tech & Two-Sided Marketplaces

## Why this topic matters
This is the "watch the neighborhood" topic: what other jobboards and two-sided marketplaces are publishing about their tech stacks, product directions, and learnings. Useful for competitive intelligence, transferable architecture lessons, and tracking regulatory shifts that affect hiring AI broadly.

Direct competitors: Indeed, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, Monster, CareerBuilder, Seek (AU), Naukri (IN), 51job (CN). Adjacent two-sided marketplaces with transferable lessons: Airbnb (matching, search, trust), Uber/Lyft/DoorDash (supply-demand balance), Etsy/eBay (long-tail discovery), Upwork/Fiverr (talent matching).

## Tracked sources
- Engineering blogs: Indeed, LinkedIn (engineering.linkedin.com), Glassdoor, Seek, Airbnb, Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Etsy, eBay, Upwork, Pinterest
- Research orgs at jobboards: LinkedIn AI, Indeed Research
- Industry pubs: ERE Media, HR Executive, SHRM tech, HR Brew, Workology
- VC / market analysis: a16z (HR tech posts), Tomasz Tunguz, jobboard earnings call coverage
- arxiv: cs.IR, cs.CY, cs.LG (filter for jobboard / employment / labor market)
- Policy: AI regulation in hiring (US, EU, UK)

## Relevance heuristics
- HIGH: direct competitor architecture/product disclosures, marketplace-design lessons that transfer to JS↔job matching, regulatory/compliance shifts, novel jobboard product mechanics
- MEDIUM: adjacent-marketplace lessons (Airbnb, Uber) on matching/trust/supply, labor-market trends data
- LOW: generic HR-tech vendor marketing, recruiting-side workflow tools far from jobboards
